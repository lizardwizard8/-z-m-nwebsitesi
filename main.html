       Favorite Art

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

The Great Wave off Kanagawa, also known as The Great Wave or simply The Wave, is a woodblock print by the Japanese artist Katsushika Hokusai. It was published sometime between 1829 and 1833 and is considered one of the most iconic works of Japanese art in the world. The print depicts a large wave that seems to be about to crash onto the shore, with Mount Fuji in the background. The wave is depicted in great detail, with its curling crest and frothy white foam. The use of the color blue in the print is particularly striking, as it is used to create a sense of depth and movement in the wave. The Great Wave is a powerful and evocative image that continues to captivate people around the world to this day. It is a testament to the skill and vision of the artist Hokusai, and serves as a reminder of the beauty and majesty of the natural world.




       Favorite Book

Thus Spoke Zarathustra by Friedrich Nietzsche

Thus Spoke Zarathustra is a book by the German philosopher Friedrich Nietzsche. It was first published in 1885 and is considered one of Nietzsche's most important and influential works. The book is a philosophical novel that follows the character of Zarathustra, a wise and solitary prophet, as he travels through the world sharing his teachings with others. The book is notable for its exploration of a number of Nietzsche's key philosophical ideas, including the concept of the "will to power" and the rejection of traditional morality. It is also famous for its use of aphorisms and paradoxes, which Nietzsche employed to challenge his readers and stimulate their thinking. Thus Spoke Zarathustra has been widely read and discussed by philosophers, scholars, and other thinkers. It continues to be one of Nietzsche's most important and enduring works. There could be some critics about the main concepts of the book but I recommend debating it with me personally.
